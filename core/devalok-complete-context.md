## 2. ORIGIN STORY & FOUNDING PHILOSOPHY

### The Trigger

Mudit Lal had been running design work for approximately four years before founding Devalok. The trigger was a realization: there was no world-class design studio coming out of Bharat that embraced its Bharatiya identity as part of its culture and celebrated it globally. Existing studios were either competing on cheapest price, were old and lacked innovative ideas, or some combination of both.

The founding conviction: India needed a studio that wasn't just following global design standards, but *setting new ones*.

### The $10 Discord Nitro Beginning

Mudit has no formal design education. He learned through experience, YouTube tutorials, and doing things. In 2020, while preparing for US college applications, he began freelancing on Discord. His first client paid $10 — and since Mudit didn't have a PayPal account, the payment came as $10 worth of Discord Nitro. The progression: $10 → $100 → $1,000 → today, where branding projects start at $10,000.

### The Starbucks Napkin Moment

Devalok's first major client was Infinity Mind Movies. Mudit met the founder while crossing the street near a pitch day event at Arizona State University. He went to see her pitch, met her again at a Starbucks, and drew her entire business plan on two Starbucks napkins — in purple ink, because Devalok's original brand color was purple. One of those napkins is still kept by Mudit.

### Why Not Take Investment

Despite having access to funds through his deep involvement in the entrepreneurial ecosystem in both India and the US, Mudit intentionally chose not to take investment. The reasoning: investors would gain equity and potentially force their way of doing things onto the studio. The values of the company are its heart and soul — if the values go away, the ability to create quality output goes away too. This was an active choice to prevent the system's values from being infiltrated.

### The Near-Failure Moment

There was one point where the business nearly failed financially. Mudit's "birthright investors" — his parents — provided a loan that allowed recovery. Deep failure has been avoided because everyone at Devalok operates with good intent and the team is highly passionate about their work.

## 3. THE NAME: DEVALOK

The story of the name is hidden within the business card itself.

**The fundamental understanding:** Every idea has three stages — birth (bud), blossoming into what it is meant to be, and death (seed pod) so that it can create space for more ideas and give birth to the next set. In Vedic civilizational culture, this process of creation (Brahma), sustenance (Vishnu), and destruction for recreation (Shiv) happens at Devalok — the celestial realm.

The name does NOT imply the studio sees itself as deity-like. It refers to the *place* where the cycle of creation happens.

**Devalok (देवलोक)** = The celestial realm where ideas take birth, blossom, and mature into completion — where they can give birth to new ideas.

## 4. CORE PHILOSOPHY & BELIEFS

### The Central Belief

> "We believe we are mediums of expression for the universe to bring its best ideas to life. This philosophy ensures we never carry the ego of 'I created this' but rather stay forever humble."

The reason the tagline says "crafting from the soul" — not from the hand or the body or the mind — is because the soul is what is truly connected to the universal energy for creation. The best work does not come *from* us. It comes *through* us. Only when we accept that we are the medium of creation for the energy that creates within the universe can we create the best work while ensuring that we are not arrogant about what we are making.

### The Three Pillars

1. **We Craft The Whole** — Entire design ecosystems from strategy to manufacturing. Not just a logo or website — the full brand identity including naming, content, visuals, motion, website, physical assets, and sometimes even supply chain.
2. **We Do It All In-House** — One multidisciplinary team, no silos. Vertical integration means Devalok handles brand content, brand voice, entire brand visuals, motion graphics, and market portrayal strategy internally.
3. **We Go Beyond the Brief** — Partners who push when potential exists. This means executing on what the client *needs*, not just what they *request*. Examples include helping Bondle (formerly Enchanted Baby) set up their entire supply chain from China to India, or advising offline-first clients against building a website when better physical assets would serve them.

### Value Creates Money, Not the Reverse

> "Value inherently brings in money. If we are able to create value through design, money will be a consequence. Money is not the goal."

The goal is the elevation of the entire design industry in India. This is why Devalok runs free educational initiatives like Gurukul and keeps all internal frameworks open for other studios to adopt.

### The Industry Needs Hundreds of Devaloks

Mudit believes a single Devalok is insufficient for the industry to thrive. What's needed is hundreds of design studios that can think in the same way or even better. This is why Devalok keeps everything open — frameworks, project flows, processes — so anyone starting a studio can skip the entire queue of building from scratch.

### Authenticity Over Acceptance

A brand should portray what it truly is, not fake it. Many businesses run away from their identity for the sake of acceptance, which makes them inauthentic. Authenticity comes from the individual's genuine commitment to the product or service. If someone is running a business solely to squeeze every last penny, that strategy is unsustainable.

### Philosophy Statements (for use in appropriate contexts)

> "The universe is always in motion — creating, sustaining, evolving. We see design as part of that rhythm: a cycle of refinement where ideas take shape, fall apart to make space for new ones, and return in their strongest form."

> "To craft is to engage with both the self and the world, to listen closely, to question deeply, and to push beyond what is known in pursuit of something meaningful. This is the spirit that shapes our practice at Devalok, where soul, intention, and execution intersect."

> "Design, for us, is never just about how something looks — it's about what it holds."

## 6. LOGO & VISUAL SYMBOLISM

The Devalok logo contains six sacred components, each rooted in Vedic philosophy:

### 1. Hand of the Universe
- In Gyan Mudra (ज्ञान मुद्रा) — gesture of knowledge and wisdom
- Alta (आलता) — bright red dye on fingertips and palm center, representing purity and divine feminine energy
- Blueish-purple tint representing Shiv, Prabhu Shri Ram, and Vishnu
- Represents the two aspects of the universe: Shiv and Shakti

### 2. Lotus of Time
- Three stages: Bud (creation), Flower (sustenance), Seed pod (re-creation)
- Represents Devalok's decision to not participate in predatory industry practices

### 3. 14 Leaves of the Loks
- The 14 realms (Lokas) of Vedic cosmology
- The 14 Manus ruling each cosmic age (Manvantara)
- The 14 types of knowledge in Vedic texts
- Upper Realms (7): Satya-loka, Tapa-loka, Jana-loka, Mahar-loka, Svar-loka, Bhuvar-loka, Bhu-loka
- Lower Realms (7): Atala, Vitala, Sutala, Talatala, Mahatala, Rasatala, Patala

### 4. Dots of the Trimurti
- Brahma (creator), Vishnu (sustainer), Shiv (destroyer/creator of space)
- Also represents Orion's Belt (Mrigashira Nakshatra)

### 5. The Sanskrit Inscription
- आत्मतः शिल्पं कृत्वा — "Crafting from the soul"
- Written below the central "SA" piece of the logo

### 6. Six-Petaled Svadhisthana Flower
- Derived from Svadhisthana Chakra (स्वाधिष्ठान) — the creativity chakra
- This is where creative thought and the ability to create is located
- Influenced by Maa Sarasvati, goddess of wisdom and creativity

**Namaskar (नमस्कार)** is the standard greeting across all Devalok communications. Use it to open formal communications, client emails, proposals, newsletters, and external content. It may be skipped in internal team messages, casual follow-ups, or when matching casual energy.

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| New client inquiry | Warm, curious, welcoming |
| Ongoing project | Collaborative, clear, energized |
| Difficult news | Honest, empathetic, solution-oriented |
| Celebration | Joyful, generous with credit, humble |
| Internal team | Direct, supportive, casual but respectful |
| Social media | Thoughtful, insight-driven, never promotional |
| Proposals | Visionary, confident, partnership-focused |

### Hindi/Sanskrit Usage Rules

With Indian clients and internal communications: use terms naturally without explanation (Namaskar, Lokwasi, Sahayak, Karyakram, Manas, Sankalan, etc.).

With international clients: use terms when they add meaning, and briefly explain on first use. Example: "We call our client handbook *Sahayak* — meaning 'one who helps.'"

**Rule:** Never force Hindi/Sanskrit for decoration. Use only when it carries meaning or creates connection.

### Signature Phrases (Use Sparingly)

These carry weight precisely because they're rare. Reserve for moments that genuinely warrant philosophical grounding:

- "From the soul, we craft."
- "Different by Design"
- "Soulful craft"

## 18. THE BREAK FRAMEWORK

Devalok's leave and attendance system. Replaces the conventional weekday/weekend model with a **fluid, trust-based break pool**. No fixed weekends, no weekdays — Lokwasis work and rest on their own rhythm within a defined annual break budget. Every calendar day counts equally.

### The Break Pool

Devalok provides **122 break days per year**, split into two halves:

| Half | Period | Days |
|------|--------|------|
| H1 | January 1 – June 30 | 61 days |
| H2 | July 1 – December 31 | 61 days |

**Origin of 122:** 52 weeks × 2 weekend days + 15 PTOs + 3 National Holidays = 122 breaks. But since there are no fixed weekends, Lokwasis choose when to use these days.

### Key Rules
- **No predefined weekends** — take breaks anytime
- **All calendar days count** — a 7-day break uses 7 days, regardless of which days of the week they fall on
- **H1→H2 carryover:** Up to 7 unused H1 days carry into H2; excess forfeited
- **Year-end cashout:** Up to 15 unused days can convert to compensation; rest forfeited
- **Protocol violations (advisory, not blocking):**
  - Breaks < 3 days: request at least 1 day before
  - Breaks ≥ 3 days: request at least 5 days before
  - Violating this flags the request but does not block it — admin sees a warning
- **Cross-boundary splits:** Breaks crossing half-year (Jun 30/Jul 1) or year (Dec 31/Jan 1) boundaries are automatically split into two linked requests, each validated against its own half's balance
- **Google Calendar sync:** All approved breaks create Out-of-Office events automatically
- **Admin overrides:** Admins with break management permission can approve, reject, edit, or cancel any break

### Mid-Year Joiners
Pro-rated allocation using ceiling (favors the Lokwasi). Each half is calculated independently based on remaining days in that half.

### National Holidays (Office Closed)
Republic Day (Jan 26), Independence Day (Aug 15), Gandhi Jayanti (Oct 2). These are the only fixed days off — everything else comes from the break pool.

### Apprentices
No fixed allocation. Breaks are approved on a case-by-case basis by admins. All other rules (calendar auth, overlap detection, protocol violations) still apply.

### Beyond BREAK (Exempt Users)
Certain users (founders, leadership) are marked as attendance-exempt — they are outside the framework entirely, cannot mark attendance, cannot request breaks, and always count as present. Their break balance is zero.

### Attendance
- **Single daily mark** — Lokwasis mark attendance once before the cutoff. This is NOT clock-in/clock-out; it's a simple "I'm here today" confirmation
- **Cutoff:** 3:00 PM IST (lokwasis must mark before this time)
- **Prerequisite:** Must have at least one active task to mark attendance
- **Break day blocking:** Cannot mark attendance on an approved break day — must cancel the break first
- **Missed attendance:** If unmarked by 11 AM the next day, the system automatically marks the Lokwasi as absent
- **Attendance corrections (RAC):** Lokwasis can request corrections for missed attendance within a 7-day lookback window, up to **15 corrections per year** (admin can grant more). Rejected corrections don't count against the quota
- **RAC threshold notifications:** In-app alerts at 50% and 25% remaining; in-app + email at 10% and 3% remaining

### Break Cancellation
- Lokwasis can cancel their own breaks **without admin permission** (admins are notified)
- **Same-day cancellation gate:** After 11 AM IST, cannot cancel today's break day (future days in a multi-day break can still be cancelled)
- **Partial cancellation:** Can remove a single day from a multi-day break — the system handles splitting the request
- **Balance restoration:** Cancelled days are automatically returned to the break pool

### Annual Lifecycle
| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Jan 1 | New year: H1 + H2 allocations created (or pro-rated for mid-year joiners) |
| Jul 1 | H1→H2 carry-forward (max 7 days) |
| Dec 15 | Year-end preview: cash-out list generated for admin review |
| Dec 31 | Year-end execute: cashout (max 15 days) + forfeit remaining + new year allocation |

## 25. INDIA'S DESIGN INDUSTRY CONTEXT

### The Historical Arc

India has celebrated design since forever — temples, scriptures, geometric patterns for specific deities. But invasions (Mughal, British colonialism) shifted national focus from *thriving* to *surviving*.

**Generation 1 (Grandparents+):** Survivability-focused. "I hope I get food to eat and ensure my family survives."
**Generation 2 (Parents/Grandparents):** Stability-focused. Government jobs became prized because they offer stability. "Can I get food three times a day and own a house?"
**Generation 3 (Current):** Quality of life-focused. This is where design fits — the top two levels of Maslow's hierarchy.

### The Maslow's Framework

A market that hasn't solved basic needs (base layers of Maslow's hierarchy) cannot be expected to value items at the top. India is a maturing market — startups now celebrate design, companies are recognized for design. Full maturity expected in 10-15 years.

### The Biggest Misconception

Clients don't understand that their product is nothing without brand strategy. In today's world, customers trust *brands* over *products*. Brand strategy creates the authenticity necessary for customers to recognize quality.

**Sankalan** · संकलन · *sun-kuh-LUN*

### Philosophy & Symbolism

**Gyan Mudra** · ज्ञान मुद्रा · *gyaan MUD-raa*
The gesture of knowledge — thumb and index finger touching, forming a circle. It represents the union of individual consciousness (Atman) with universal wisdom (Brahman). Featured prominently in the Devalok logo as the hand's primary gesture.

**Alta** · आलता · *AAL-taa*
A bright red dye traditionally applied to fingertips and the center of the palm by women in South Asia. It represents purity, auspiciousness, and divine feminine energy. Depicted in the Devalok logo on the hand's fingertips.

**Trimurti** · त्रिमूर्ति · *tri-MOOR-tee*
The three forms — Brahma (the creator), Vishnu (the sustainer), and Shiv (the destroyer who creates space for renewal). This cycle of creation, sustenance, and transformation is the philosophical engine behind Devalok's approach to design. Represented in the logo by three dots, which also symbolize Orion's Belt (Mrigashira Nakshatra).

**Svadhisthana** · स्वाधिष्ठान · *svaa-dhish-THAA-nuh*
The creativity chakra — the second energy center in yogic tradition, associated with creative expression, flow, and emotional intelligence. The six-petaled flower in the Devalok logo is derived from this chakra, and the studio's six-pointed star tessellation pattern is based on the same form.

**Brahman** · ब्रह्मन् · *BRUH-mun*
The ultimate reality or source in Hindu philosophy — the infinite, formless ground from which all creation emerges. In Devalok's philosophy, creativity is understood as flowing from this higher source. This underpins the "mediums, not masters" ethos — the belief that we channel creative energy rather than claiming ownership of it.

**Shakti** · शक्ति · *SHUK-tee*
Divine feminine energy — the active, creative power of the universe. Paired with Shiv (pure consciousness), Shakti represents the dynamic force that brings form to the formless. Represented in the logo through the symbolism of the hand.

**Panchang** · पंचांग · *pun-CHAANG*
The traditional Hindu lunar calendar system, literally meaning "five limbs" — referring to the five elements of time it tracks (tithi, vara, nakshatra, yoga, karana). Used by Devalok to determine the date of Lokutsav and for cultural and ceremonial planning.

**Loka** · लोक · *LOKE*
Realm or world. In Vedic cosmology, there are 14 Lokas — seven upper realms and seven lower realms, representing the full spectrum of existence. The 14 leaves in the Devalok logo represent these 14 realms. The word is also the root of Devalok itself (Deva + Loka = celestial realm).

### What Devalok Sounds Like

**❌ Not Devalok:**
> "We leverage cutting-edge design methodologies to optimize brand performance and drive scalable growth solutions."

**✅ Devalok:**

### How to Talk About Devalok

**One Line:** "We're a design and strategy studio that helps thoughtful brands tell their story and build their presence — from identity and packaging to websites and beyond."

**Paragraph:** "Devalok is a design and strategy studio that works with founders who care about meaning. We don't just design logos or websites — we build entire brand ecosystems, from strategy to manufacturing. Our philosophy is 'From the soul, we craft' — we see ourselves as partners in bringing meaningful ideas to life."

Never pitch. Partner. A proposal is not a sales document — it's the beginning of a conversation.

## 31. KEY LESSONS & HARD-WON WISDOM

These are things Mudit has learned through experience running Devalok:

1. **Never skip the hiring process.** Every time lateral hiring was done (bypassing the standard application → screening → minimum 3 interviews → decision flow), it went wrong. This mistake was made twice.

2. **A studio cannot be world-class on a team of freelancers.** A committed team is necessary to maintain standards and methodology.

3. **90% of client issues are avoided by choosing the right clients in the first place.** The first gate — gauging how serious and authentic a founder is — prevents most downstream problems.

4. **Scope creep is managed by increasing price, not by absorbing it.** Or by saying no to clients who don't understand design's value.

5. **Vertical stacking slows growth but builds lasting value.** The tradeoff is worth it for finding teammates committed to the long-term vision.

6. **Physical assets create connections that digital cannot.** Lakhs spent on printing Sahayak and Manas is an intentional investment in tactility and trust.

7. **Value creates money, not the reverse.** If the focus is on creating value through design, money follows as a consequence.

8. **The founder's authenticity sets the ceiling for the brand.** If a founder doesn't genuinely care about the problem they're solving, the brand work will suffer no matter how good the studio is.

9. **You should be happy you got to work with us** — not the reverse. Not arrogance, but confidence born from quality of delivery.

10. **Open everything.** Keeping frameworks, processes, and knowledge open strengthens the entire industry, which ultimately strengthens Devalok.
