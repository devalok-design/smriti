# Devalok Glossary

### A complete guide to the language of Devalok Design and Strategy Studio

**Version:** 1.0.0
**Last Updated:** 21 March 2026

> **आत्मतः शिल्पं कृत्वा** — "From the soul, we craft."

At Devalok, language is never accidental. The words we use — drawn from Sanskrit, Hindi, and our own studio traditions — carry the same intentionality as our design work. Each term is chosen because it holds meaning, not because it sounds impressive.

This glossary is a guide for our clients, collaborators, and anyone entering the Devalok world. If you encounter a word here that's unfamiliar, it's because we believe the right word — even if it takes a moment to learn — says something no substitute can.

---

## Studio Identity

The foundational terms that define who we are.

**Devalok** · देवलोक · *day-vuh-LOKE*
The celestial realm — a place of creation, sustenance, and re-creation. In Hindu philosophy, Devalok is where ideas take birth, blossom, and mature into completion, where they can give birth to new ideas. This eternal cycle of creation, sustenance, and transformation is the foundation of our studio's identity. Founded 23 August 2024.

**Lokwasi** · लोकवासी · *loke-VAH-see*
Inhabitant of the Lok. This is what we call every member of the Devalok team — not employees, but inhabitants of a shared creative world. The plural is *Lokwasis*. The studio's internal motto is: *For the Lokwasis, by the Lokwasis.*

**Devadoot** · देवदूत · *day-vuh-DOOT*
Divine messenger — one who carries the message of the divine realm. Devadoot is the name of Devalok's AI assistant, which embodies the studio's voice, values, and operational knowledge across every interaction.

**Padmavarna** · पद्मवर्ण · *pud-muh-VAR-nuh*
Lotus color. The soul color of Devalok — a specific shade of pink (#D33163) that represents the studio's visual identity. Registered at color-register.org. Used purposefully for emphasis and accent, never as a background fill.

**Namaskar** · नमस्कार · *nuh-muh-SKAR*
A traditional greeting meaning "I bow to the divine in you." It carries respect, warmth, and acknowledgment of the other person's inherent dignity. Namaskar is the standard greeting across all Devalok formal communications.

**Āt̤matah̤ Śilpaṁ Kṛtvā** · आत्मतः शिल्पं कृत्वा · *aat-muh-tuh SHIL-pum KRIT-vaa*
From the soul, we craft. Devalok's core philosophy — the belief that true creativity flows from a higher source, and that we are conduits of that expression, not its masters. Used sparingly and intentionally, reserved for high-impact moments. Never in routine communications.

## Publications & Knowledge Systems

The named documents and publications that carry our knowledge.

**Lokgranth** · लोक ग्रंथ · *loke-GRUNTH*
Book of the Lok. Devalok's internal team handbook containing operating principles, collaboration philosophy, culture guidelines, and the collective wisdom of the studio. An ever-evolving document that grows with the team. Internal only — not shared with clients.

**Karyakram** · कार्यक्रम · *kar-ya-KRAM*
Program or plan. The task management system within Karm that serves as the single source of truth for every project's work. Projects are organized into stages (kanban columns) containing tasks with assignees, due dates, priority, and status. Tasks support threaded messages, file attachments, deliverables with a review cycle, and activity logs. Originally maintained as a Google Docs record per project, Karyakram has since been rebuilt as a full system within Karm.

**Sahayak** · सहायक · *suh-HAA-yuk*
One who helps. The client handbook given to every new client at the start of an engagement. It walks them through Devalok's philosophy, services, process, and what to expect from the collaboration. The name reflects exactly what it's designed to do — help.

**Mānas** · मानस · *MAA-nus*
Mind or heart. Devalok's annual portfolio book — not just a showcase of finished work, but a collection of stories, process, and the thinking behind every project. Shared with prospects, clients, and partners. Each volume captures a chapter of the studio's evolution.

**Sankalan** · संकलन · *sun-kuh-LUN*
Collection or compilation. Devalok's monthly newsletter — a curated digest of studio updates, thinking, and creative explorations. Distributed via sankalan@devalok.in.

## Platforms & Systems

The digital tools and platforms we build and operate.

**Karm** · कर्म · *KARM*
Action or work. In Vedic philosophy, Karm is the principle that every action has consequence and meaning. At Devalok, Karm is the workplace operations platform — tracking attendance, payroll, projects, tasks, and team operations. It also houses internal AI agents and serves as the studio's digital nervous system. Accessed at karm.devalok.in.

**Sarathi** · सारथी · *SAA-ruh-thee*
Charioteer or guide. In the Mahabharata, the Sarathi is the trusted navigator who steers the chariot — most famously, Lord Krishna serving as Arjuna's charioteer. At Devalok, Sarathi is an AI-native platform built for university innovation ecosystems, currently in Live Alpha development.

**Devadoot (AI Agent)** · देवदूत
Within the Karm platform, Devadoot functions as the master orchestrator AI agent — coordinating between other agents and the team's operational needs.

**Sutradhar** · सूत्रधार · *SOOT-ruh-dhaar*
The one who holds the threads. In Sanskrit theatre, the Sutradhar is the narrator who weaves the story together and ensures coherence. Within Karm, Sutradhar is the AI agent responsible for project health monitoring — watching the threads of every active project to flag risks before they become problems.

**Sanchalak** · संचालक · *sun-CHAA-luk*
Director or conductor — one who manages and orchestrates. Within Karm, Sanchalak is the AI agent that provides workload advisory and resource balancing, ensuring no Lokwasi is stretched too thin.

**Śilpa Sūtra** · शिल्प सूत्र · *SHIL-puh SOOT-ruh*
The thread of craft. In the Vedic tradition, Sūtras are concise rules that encode knowledge (as in the Yoga Sūtras). Śilpa means craft or art. Together, Śilpa Sūtra is Devalok's design system — the codified rules, tokens, components, and patterns that govern the studio's visual craft. The name connects directly back to the founding philosophy of *āt̤matah̤ śilpaṁ kṛtvā*.

## Events & Initiatives

The gatherings and programs that bring our community together.

**Lokutsav** · लोकउत्सव · *loke-UT-suv*
Festival of the Lok. Devalok's annual birth anniversary celebration, dated according to the Panchang (Hindu lunar calendar) rather than the Gregorian calendar. A day to reflect on the studio's journey and celebrate the team.

**Aabhaar** · आभार · *aa-BHAAR*
Gratitude. Our client appreciation events — evenings dedicated not to business, but to the people who trust us with their brands and visions. As we say: "Tonight isn't about business, it's about the people in this room."

**Gurukul** · गुरुकुल · *goo-roo-KUL*
The teacher's home — the traditional Indian model of education where learning happens through immersion, mentorship, and direct relationship between teacher and student. Devalok Gurukul is our design education initiative, making quality design education accessible to a wider community.

**Devsabha** · देवसभा · *dev-SUB-haa*
Assembly of the divine. The studio's weekly team meeting, held every Thursday morning. A space where every Lokwasi shares their current bandwidth, project updates, and any blocks they're facing. Named to reflect that the gathering is more than administrative — it's a collective check-in on the health of the studio.

## Philosophy & Symbolism

The deeper ideas and symbols woven into our identity.

**Gyan Mudra** · ज्ञान मुद्रा · *gyaan MUD-raa*
The gesture of knowledge — thumb and index finger touching, forming a circle. It represents the union of individual consciousness (Atman) with universal wisdom (Brahman). Featured prominently in the Devalok logo as the hand's primary gesture.

**Alta** · आलता · *AAL-taa*
A bright red dye traditionally applied to fingertips and the center of the palm by women in South Asia. It represents purity, auspiciousness, and divine feminine energy. Depicted in the Devalok logo on the hand's fingertips.

**Trimurti** · त्रिमूर्ति · *tri-MOOR-tee*
The three forms — Brahma (the creator), Vishnu (the sustainer), and Shiv (the destroyer who creates space for renewal). This cycle of creation, sustenance, and transformation is the philosophical engine behind Devalok's approach to design. Represented in the logo by three dots, which also symbolize Orion's Belt (Mrigashira Nakshatra).

**Svadhisthana** · स्वाधिष्ठान · *svaa-dhish-THAA-nuh*
The creativity chakra — the second energy center in yogic tradition, associated with creative expression, flow, and emotional intelligence. The six-petaled flower in the Devalok logo is derived from this chakra, and the studio's six-pointed star tessellation pattern is based on the same form.

**Brahman** · ब्रह्मन् · *BRUH-mun*
The ultimate reality or source in Hindu philosophy — the infinite, formless ground from which all creation emerges. In Devalok's philosophy, creativity is understood as flowing from this higher source. This underpins the "mediums, not masters" ethos — the belief that we channel creative energy rather than claiming ownership of it.

**Shakti** · शक्ति · *SHUK-tee*
Divine feminine energy — the active, creative power of the universe. Paired with Shiv (pure consciousness), Shakti represents the dynamic force that brings form to the formless. Represented in the logo through the symbolism of the hand.

**Panchang** · पंचांग · *pun-CHAANG*
The traditional Hindu lunar calendar system, literally meaning "five limbs" — referring to the five elements of time it tracks (tithi, vara, nakshatra, yoga, karana). Used by Devalok to determine the date of Lokutsav and for cultural and ceremonial planning.

**Loka** · लोक · *LOKE*
Realm or world. In Vedic cosmology, there are 14 Lokas — seven upper realms and seven lower realms, representing the full spectrum of existence. The 14 leaves in the Devalok logo represent these 14 realms. The word is also the root of Devalok itself (Deva + Loka = celestial realm).

**The 14 Loks** — The 14 leaves in the logo represent the 14 realms of Vedic cosmology:

*Upper Realms:* Satya-loka, Tapa-loka, Jana-loka, Mahar-loka, Svar-loka, Bhuvar-loka, Bhu-loka (Earth).
*Lower Realms:* Atala, Vitala, Sutala, Talatala, Mahatala, Rasatala, Patala.

## Logo Components

The Devalok logo contains six sacred components, each rooted in Vedic philosophy.

**Hand of the Universe** — In Gyan Mudra (gesture of knowledge and wisdom), with Alta on fingertips and palm center. The blueish-purple tint represents Shiv, Prabhu Shri Ram, and Vishnu. Represents the two aspects of the universe: Shiv and Shakti.

**Lotus of Time** — Three stages: Bud (creation), Flower (sustenance), Seed pod (re-creation). Represents Devalok's commitment to not participating in predatory industry practices, and symbolizes the natural cycle all things pass through.

**14 Leaves of the Loks** — The 14 realms of Vedic cosmology, the 14 Manus ruling each cosmic age, and the 14 types of knowledge in Vedic texts.

**Dots of the Trimurti** — Brahma (creator), Vishnu (sustainer), Shiv (destroyer/creator of space). Also represents Orion's Belt (Mrigashira Nakshatra).

**The Sanskrit Inscription** — आत्मतः शिल्पं कृत्वा — "Crafting from the soul." Instills humility, purpose, and reverence.

**Six-Petaled Svadhisthana Flower** — Derived from the Svadhisthana Chakra (creativity chakra), influenced by Maa Sarasvati, goddess of wisdom and creativity.

## Design & Brand Vocabulary

Terms specific to our visual identity system.

**Monogram** — The icon-only version of the Devalok logo — the hand with lotus, without any accompanying text. Used for favicons, social avatars, and compact brand applications.

**Shell** — The circular container that encloses the Devalok monogram. Used for social media profiles and digital applications.

**Coin** — An alternate circular container style for the monogram.

**Wordmark** — The "DEVALOK" text logo, used alongside or independently of the monogram. The primary text-based brand mark.

**DASS** — Design and Strategy Studio. An alternate wordmark that spells out the studio's full descriptor. Used in formal and institutional contexts.

**Shloka** · श्लोक · *SHLOKE*
A Sanskrit verse. In Devalok's brand system, this refers to the wordmark version that features the Sanskrit inscription (आत्मतः शिल्पं कृत्वा) in Devanagari script.

## Roles & Structure

How we organize ourselves. At Devalok, roles are responsibilities, not ranks. Leadership is rotational — a DA might lead one project and assist on another.

**Apprentice** — A Lokwasi in training. Focus is on skill-building, exposure to real work, and learning through doing alongside experienced team members.

**ADA (Assistant Design Associate)** — A Lokwasi who works independently, understands the right process, and is gaining cross-disciplinary experience.

**DA (Design Associate)** — A Lokwasi who has developed expertise in at least one area with cross-disciplinary awareness. Mentors ADAs and fellow DAs.

**DD (Design Director)** — Provides creative direction across multiple teams. Elevates quality and consistency across the studio's output.

**Content Associate** — Specializes in copywriting, content strategy, and brand voice.

**Operations Associate / Apprentice** — Focuses on operations, administration, and coordination.

**US Representative** — Manages US client relations and operations from Phoenix, Arizona.

**Director of Stewardship** — The guardian of Devalok's people and culture. Responsible for ensuring that every Lokwasi is supported, heard, and growing.

**PAC (People and Culture)** — The department responsible for team operations, HR, culture-building, and the wellbeing of every Lokwasi. Reachable at pac@devalok.in.

## Process & Client Terms

The language of how we work with our clients.

**Discovery Call** — The first conversation with a potential client. Not a sales pitch — a genuine exploration of whether our philosophies and goals align. Led by the Founder. Bookable at talk.devalok.in.

**Brand Workshop** — A deep-dive session into a client's brand story, values, audience, and direction. Conducted collaboratively using a custom FigJam board. The foundation from which all design work grows.

**Deck 01** — The philosophy and overview presentation. An introduction to who Devalok is, how we think, and why we work the way we do. Shared during the first client call.

**Deck 02** — The custom brand workshop presentation, tailored to each client's context and needs. Includes an onboarding questionnaire. Shared before the onboarding call.

**Deck 03** — The phase-wise timeline presentation. A clear roadmap of the project's journey from start to completion. Shared after the onboarding call and workshop.

**The Five-Stage Process:**
1. *Discovery Call* — Exchange questions, listen openly, check alignment.
2. *Onboarding & Brand Workshop* — Deep-dive into the brand's story and creative direction.
3. *Creative Development* — Concepts developed, tested, and refined with client feedback.
4. *Brand Applications* — Identity extended into websites, packaging, campaigns, and experiences.
5. *Official Launch* — Every asset, platform, and channel prepared for rollout.

## Frameworks & Policies

The internal systems that govern how we work and grow.

**The Break Framework (TBF)** — Devalok's leave and rest policy — 122 break days per year with complete flexibility. No predefined weekends, no rigid structure. Trust-based and designed to honor creative cycles. Formula: 52 weeks × 2 weekend days + 15 PTOs + 3 National Holidays = 122 breaks. Accessible at tbf.devalok.in.

**2A3B** — Devalok's feedback framework. A structured approach to giving critique that builds people up:
- **Ask** questions — understand the thinking before reacting.
- **Acknowledge** what works and why.
- **Be timely** — give feedback when it can still shape the outcome.
- **Be specific** — explain *why* something isn't working.
- **Be the guide** — point toward solutions, not just problems.

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Explain Terms

| Audience | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Indian clients** | Use terms naturally, no explanation needed |
| **Internal team** | Use freely |
| **International clients** | Explain on first use, use sparingly |
| **Public content** | Brief explanation in parentheses or as footnote |

### Example Explanations

> "We call our client handbook *Sahayak* — meaning 'one who helps.'"

> "This is *Aabhaar*, our evening of gratitude."

> "Our philosophy, *आत्मतः शिल्पं कृत्वा*, translates to 'From the soul, we craft.'"

### The Rule

Never force Hindi or Sanskrit for decoration. Use it only when it carries meaning or creates connection.
