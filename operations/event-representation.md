# Event Representation Guide

How to represent Devalok at external events — networking meetups, conferences, trade shows, exhibitions. This is not a script. It is the posture, energy, and field knowledge that anyone carrying the Devalok name should internalize before walking into a room.

---

## 1. The Devalok Energy

The single most important thing: **no desperation.**

Anything that carries the energy of "pick me," "work with us," or trying to convince the person in front of you is not the Devalok energy. The Devalok energy is: *we do X, Y, and Z, and we are exceptional at it. If that is something you are looking for, let's connect.*

- Do not pitch. Do not sell. Do not chase.
- Convey competence and value, not need.
- Devalok has already crossed its capacity of clients it can take and is working on scaling — the value addition is for them, not for us.
- Be joyful. Keep a genuine smile — not a performative one. Devalok representatives are naturally warm people; this should not need to be faked.

---

## 2. Table & Stall Behavior

Do not "man the stall." Materials — copies of *Sankalan*, *Sahayak*, *Manas* — sit on the table. Let them do the work.

- Let people pick things up on their own.
- If someone picks up a publication, reads it, lingers for a few seconds — then walk up and introduce yourself as the Devalok representative.
- If a group gathers, feel free to offer: *"If you have any questions, feel free to ask."*
- The approach is always from a place of calm presence, never hovering.

---

## 3. Attire

Dress smartly. Follow whatever the event's dress code dictates.

- Indian wear (*kurta*, *sari*) is preferred where appropriate, but this is an individual decision based on how confidently you can carry it — wearing traditional attire in a professional setting sometimes draws side-eyes, so it has to feel natural and strong on you.
- If in doubt, smart professional attire is always safe.

---

## 4. The Elevator Pitch

When someone asks "So what is Devalok?", the natural response flow:

> Devalok is a full-service design studio focused on branding. We help brands — both existing and new — with everything from naming their company or product, to developing the entire brand strategy, logo, visual identity, packaging, websites, apps — everything. We are a one-stop solution for companies looking to establish a brand for themselves.

People will often follow up with: *"Oh, so it's a marketing company?"*

The correction is important but should not be condescending:

> No, we don't do marketing. We give birth to the brand that *leads* to great marketing. Without a great brand, there is no great marketing.

The key distinction to land: Devalok builds the foundation that marketing is built on. Great marketing requires a great brand first.

---

## 5. Guiding Conversations

### Make them feel interesting

The most critical conversational skill: genuine curiosity about the other person. Ask about their work, the problems they are solving within their brand, what they are building. Let them talk — people love to talk about themselves.

- Spend 2–5 minutes on their story before pivoting to what Devalok does.
- Adapt your pitch to their context. If they build physical products, talk about packaging and e-commerce. If they are a services company, talk about brand identity and websites.

### Offer value, not fluff

If you can think through a genuine suggestion for them, offer it. But only if you are confident in the suggestion — a half-baked answer does more damage than saying nothing. It is fine to say *"That's interesting — I'd need to think about that more, but let's stay connected."*

### Know when to exit

Not every conversation is worth extended time. If it is going nowhere after a few minutes, gracefully move on. Your time at the event is limited.

---

## 6. Pricing

Be blunt. No dancing around it.

> Our charges for brand identity start at $10,000.

If someone says they only want a website:
- Assess whether they already have a strong existing brand.
- If yes (rare): offer to set up a meeting to discuss further.
- If no (most cases): recommend working on the brand first before the website. *"I'd recommend we work on the brand before moving to the website — the website will be significantly stronger for it."*

---

## 7. Evaluating Someone's Brand On the Spot

People will ask for your opinion on their existing brand. Instead of giving a direct opinion:

1. Ask them to explain their brand — what it stands for, who it is for, what they want it to communicate.
2. Then look at what the brand actually shows visually.
3. Point out the contrast. If they say "luxury" but the textures, business card, and visual identity feel budget — that gap is the insight.

This is more credible than subjective criticism. You are letting the disconnect speak for itself.

---

## 8. Brand Identity vs. Brand Visual Identity

This distinction comes up often and must be stated clearly:

| Term | What It Covers |
|------|----------------|
| **Brand Visual Identity** | Logo, colors, typography, brand patterns, letterheads, business cards, presentations — the visual layer. |
| **Brand Identity** | Everything above, plus brand strategy (values, vision, mission), brand voice, brand architecture (how multiple products/sub-brands relate). |

Devalok does both. Most clients need both.

## 10. The Branding Process (When Asked)

> Every branding engagement starts with a brand workshop — a one- to three-hour deep dive where we get to know you, your brand, and your business. Before that, there is usually an initial call with Mudit to understand the business, after which we put together a proposal outlining how we can help — today, tomorrow, and in the future.

Keep it simple. The brand workshop is the anchor of the process; everything flows from there.

## 11. AI at Devalok

If asked about AI usage:

- Yes, Devalok uses AI — but as an **amplification tool**, not a replacement for human work.
- The ideological thought process and attention to detail remain human-driven.
- AI amplifies quality — a person who creates excellent work will create more of it. It does not compensate for a lack of taste or skill.
- Primary tool: **Claude AI**, on a team subscription that protects client data from being used to train models.
- Also use **Gemini** and **Pro** for visual imagination.

## 12. Networking Without Business Cards

If physical business cards are unavailable:

- Have your LinkedIn QR code saved to your phone gallery for quick access.
- Print the QR code and display it on the table — fold card-stock into a standing display.
- Collect their business cards as well.
- The back page of *Manas* also has contact information as a fallback.

---

## 13. Personal Conduct & Safety

- **Trust your instincts.** If a conversation gives off uncomfortable energy, walk away. There is zero obligation to continue speaking to anyone — regardless of their title, status, or wealth.
- It is natural for older attendees to view younger representatives warmly ("like my daughter"). That is fine. Use it as a bridge, not a limitation — when the conversation turns to work, switch to confident, knowledgeable delivery.
- **Do not volunteer personal details** like university or student status unless specifically asked. It can dilute the professional confidence the other person has in the company.
- Focus energy on the work, the portfolio, the achievements. Let those speak.
