## 01 · DIVINI: Brewing Divinity

| | |
|---|---|
| **Brand** | DIVINI |
| **Client** | Mason Domecq |
| **Project** | Branding Identity Design, Packaging, Apparel Design, Website Design |
| **Status** | Ongoing |

*[Opening image: DIVINI Apple Pie can with glass — ornate terracotta/red Art Nouveau-inspired illustration on white can, photographed by Kyle Shipley (Figure 1.01)]*

### Introduction

DIVINI is more than just a brand; it is an embodiment of a philosophy — living with intention. At the heart of Divini lies Mason Domecq's vision, which aims to blend health and elegance into the modern lifestyle. DIVINI offers a vibrant line of kombucha drinks, a probiotic beverage that combines ancient fermentation techniques with contemporary flavors. Each drop is designed not only to quench thirst but also to elevate daily rituals into highly meaningful experiences.

Mason's journey to creating DIVINI began with wanting to change how people perceive health drinks. As a self-proclaimed "lover of balance," he noticed a glaring gap in the market: beverages were either too clinical in their branding or overly indulgent. DIVINI sought to bridge this gap by appealing to more health-conscious individuals and those who value visual aesthetic experiences. Hence, DIVINI is made with carefully blended organic ingredients like tea, fruit, & herbs, and the fermentation process ensures a natural fizz that supports gut health.

DIVINI wasn't always known as DIVINI. When we first met Mason, it was called Sooty Drinks. In our early branding process, we recommended this name change to align with Mason's vision of making it a part of diverse lifestyles. The name DIVINI derives from Divine, which reflects the commitment of this brand to connect people with their divine selves and foster a lifestyle grounded in intention.

> DIVINI positions itself as more than just a drink — it becomes a lifestyle choice for those who seek balance, nourishment, & sophistication in their everyday lives.

### The DIVINI Story

DIVINI wanted a can that could look good in the hands of a teenager on a skateboard, at a corporate office, and even in an NBA player's hand. The visual identity and illustrations combine their elegance with the fun of the can to create the essence of DIVINI. Whether you're drawn to DIVINI for its health benefits or aesthetics, the can itself becomes an invitation — tangible proof that drinking probiotic tea can be both healthy and aesthetic. After all, it's not just what you drink but how it feels to hold it, see it and live it.

In every aspect, DIVINI tells a cohesive story. It's a brand that doesn't just sell probiotic tea; it sells a way of being. Mason's vision, coupled with our creative design and thoughtful community engagement, makes DIVINI a standout in a crowded market. It's not just a drink; it's a vibe, a movement, and an ode to the power of living with intention.

### Tea in a 'Can' That Started It All

While brainstorming for visuals for DIVINI, we debated on what style would fit the 'spiritual' and 'divine' aspect of the brand. We looked into archives of old book prints where we noticed the intricate borders surrounding the neat calligraphy. The illustrative technique was used in abundance in books during the 17th and 18th century all around Europe. These included Gothic Borders, Celtic knots, Herringbone, etc.

But their aesthetic was slightly too aged for DIVINI. That is, until we came across Glasgow-style art pioneered by Rennie Mackintosh, which revolutionized commercial art from the 1890s to around 1914. It was recognized by many as a Scottish manifestation of the Art Nouveau movement. The DIVINI borders, thus, came to be embellished with organic motifs inspired by the Glasgow Style to create something sublime but contemporary.

The DIVINI logo is made using 'Gangster' typeface by RuneStudio. It's a serif display font; simple and elegant.

*[Visual elements: Can design flat with annotated nutritional facts, ingredient callouts, Gothic/Celtic border illustrations, probiotic bacteria icons. Product photography by Cameron Moghin showing lifestyle shots — outdoor settings, casual fashion context, 6-pack box on grass (Figures 1.03–1.09)]*

*[Digital mockup: DIVINI Apple Pie Can floating in clouds with glass — ethereal product visualization (Figure 1.07)]*

### The DIVINI Kimono: A Wearable Story

Our creative vision didn't just stick to packaging and design but extended into fashion with the creation of a kimono. Rather than defaulting to commonplace promotional items like t-shirts, the team envisioned a garment that would capture the essence of DIVINI's visual language while offering genuine utility. This decision reflects the brand's broader philosophy: everything associated with DIVINI should be both artful and practical.

The creative process began with combining traditional silhouettes with modern, everyday functions. Kimonos, known for their craftsmanship and cultural value, became the ideal canvas for DIVINI's mix of history and modern style. We slightly departed from the brand's usual black-and-white palette by choosing a rich, dark blue base. This color was selected for its ability to blend into various occasions: it can look relaxed and informal in a daytime gathering or suitably elevated during an evening event.

The kimono brings DIVINI's aesthetic to life, extending it beyond their probiotic tea. Every detail — from the versatile hue and the fabric to the screen-printed artwork — reflects the brand's focus on thoughtful design and practicality. This understated design encourages wearers to make it a part of their daily lives, transforming it from merely a collectible to a versatile wardrobe staple.

*[Photography: DIVINI Kimono in dark navy blue — collar close-up showing woven DIVINI label, front and back shots worn by Mason Domecq, screen-printed Art Nouveau illustration on back panel. Photographed by Kyle Shipley. Manufactured by Maev Overseas. (Figures 1.11–1.18)]*

### The DIVINI Lifestyle Apparel

*[Digital flat sketches: DIVINI Unisex Button-down Shirt — front view (white collar, black body, DIVINI text on sleeves) and back view (large DIVINI wordmark along bottom hem). Lifestyle photography of Mason wearing the shirt on a spiral staircase. (Figures 1.19–1.24)]*

### Taking Divini's Philosophy Online: A Seamless Digital Experience

DIVINI's website, livedivini.com, takes the brand's philosophy online with a clean, thoughtful design that feels both elegant and approachable. While the brand's packaging leans on Art Nouveau, Celtic, and Gothic design elements, the online space opts for understated elegance, using white space, gentle animations, and a streamlined user interface.

From the start, the landing page grabs attention with a simple yet striking animation: a DIVINI can gently descends from a softly lit sky, coming to rest as if placed there just for you. As you scroll, the can moves gently downward, eventually resting on a virtual "ground," giving way to more detailed brand and product information. This smooth vertical animation creates a sense of wonder and positions DIVINI's probiotic tea as something almost celestial yet entirely within reach.

This small animation sets the site's tone — minimalist, seamless, and focused on making every interaction effortless. Dark text on a light background maintains readability, while small bursts of accent color and carefully placed icons mirror DIVINI's broader brand identity.

### Information Architecture

*[Diagram: DIVINI website IA showing diamond-shaped flowchart from topical geographic icons down through Price & CTA, Subscription, How it is made, Description, Brief, Images, Apple Pie products to Social Links and Contact Form (Figure 1.27)]*

DIVINI's website leverages a clear, step-by-step architecture to guide visitors from discovery to purchase. A concise main menu — Home, Products, Subscription — keeps navigation simple. Deeper brand narratives remain tucked in expandable sections, preserving a clean layout.

### A Simple and Intuitive Subscription System

The subscription system, central to livedivini.com, had to integrate smoothly with this minimalist design. Users can explore flavors, bundle sizes, and delivery options step by step, making the process simpler and more clear. Each choice builds on the last, so subscribers never feel overwhelmed. Subtle animations like button highlights guide them, while the minimalist layout focuses on the products.

## 02 · Coffee Plantation: A Legacy Revived

| | |
|---|---|
| **Brand** | Coffee Plantation |

| **Project** | Brand Identity Design |
| **Status** | Ongoing |

*[Opening image: Kaizen branded box truck mockup on dark navy background (Figure 4.01 — note: appears to be a production error, truck is from Kaizen section)]*

### Introduction — Reviving The Soul of Coffee Houses

Coffee Plantation is a place where the world meets — where stories are shared over a cup, and connections are brewed alongside coffee.

Coffee Plantation is a name that has long been intertwined with the café culture of Scottsdale, Arizona. Known for its rich history, it was one of the first coffee houses to introduce the idea of a dedicated coffee space where conversations brewed as effortlessly as the coffee. For decades, it served as a cornerstone of community life, where people gathered to exchange ideas, share stories, or enjoy solitude over a warm coffee.

Despite its significance, Coffee Plantation's visual identity had become outdated. It did not capture the warmth and vibrancy of the café's legacy. Over the years, as ownership changed hands, the café's branding lacked consistency, and modern competitors overshadowed its essence.

The design brief presented to us was clear yet deeply layered. Coffee Plantation wanted to reclaim its position as a community hub — a space where people didn't just grab coffee on the go but stayed to engage in meaningful interactions.

### Reviving the Soul of Coffeehouses

Coffeehouses have always been more than just places to get a good cup of coffee and bagels. Historically, they served as social hubs where traders, writers, artists, and intellectuals gathered. People talked about everything from politics and art to philosophy and world events. Over time, cafes evolved to accommodate changing lifestyles. They became havens for those seeking a peaceful space to work, read, or relax.

Coffee Plantation, as a legacy brand with a deep connection to its community, attracts a diverse consumer base. Hence, we wanted the brand to speak to this variety of consumers while staying rooted in its heritage.

### The Philosophy of Building a Logo: From Concept to Paper

There are many factors involved in designing a brand image — culture, people, trends, philosophy, geography, etc. Coffee Plantation stood firm in the middle of all this. Its legacy did not concern itself with Instagrammability or fleeting trends. It needed to be new, yes, but also timeless so that it could blend seamlessly into the daily lives of people who have known it for decades. It needed to express a sense of space, leisure, and belonging.

So, we took a page from the book of designers who created logos for art galleries and museum. Since locations like these want to encourage a sense of 'vast spaces where time passes slowly', their logos usually reflect that. Be it Toledo Museum of Art in Ohio, National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art in Korea, Art Gallery of New South Wales in UK, all share the same sensibilities.

To further distance "Plantation" from its historical baggage, we introduced subtle symbolic elements that redefined its meaning. The typography of "Plantation" incorporated abstract design elements inspired by a communal table. This was achieved by stylizing the crossbars of the "T" and the curves of the "A" to subtly evoke the imagery of a table — an inviting space for gathering and connection.

*[Logo evolution: Multiple Coffee Plantation logo drafts showing progression from mechanical to refined. Final logo with table-inspired letterforms. Brand Visual Table-top showing coffee moka pot and cup integrated into the table motif. Tagline: "WHERE THE WORLD MEETS, ONE CUP AT A TIME" (Figures 2.05–2.07)]*

### Color and Type

*[Color palette: Warm, earthy tones — Whipped Cream (#fefbf0), Iced Latte (#eae8ea), Minted Brew (#bdd4c7), Cinnamon Spice (#e4602d), Peach (#f2cdb2), Vanilla (#f9f5e2), Forest Brew (#2c4944), Matcha (#7aa355), Light Green (#aeca72), Dark Matcha (#4c5e4a), Cocoa Brown (#3e2b1d), Chestnut Brown (#583c27) (Figure 2.09)]*

### Illustrative Style: A Conscious Choice for Sophistication and Timelessness

Illustration played a pivotal role in the rebranding of Coffee Plantation, bridging the gap between its rich heritage and its modern identity. The choice of an illustrative style was far from arbitrary — it was a calculated decision that reflected the café's essence as a space for connection, thoughtfulness, and community.

When we first conceptualized the illustrative elements, one thing was clear: we wanted to avoid literal representations. Literal illustrations, while often easier to interpret, can risk feeling generic or overly casual — qualities that did not align with Coffee Plantation's legacy.

### Introduction — From Enchanted Baby to Bondle

Bondle isn't just another baby brand! It's an idea or commune of love and parenthood. It's the joy of building a beautiful bond between parent and child.

Bondle is an innovative brand that prioritizes creating memorable experiences for parents, emphasizing reimagining the world of parenting through thoughtful design. At its core, Bondle's mission is to empower parents with innovative tools to enhance sleep, strengthen bonds, and support family well-being.

The brand name reflects the foundational values of connection and nurture, blending the words "bond" and "bundle" to capture the joy and intimacy of parenthood. Bondle acknowledges that parenthood comes in many forms, whether supported by grandparents, single parents, or diverse family structures. This philosophy drives the brand's commitment to making inclusive design choices that resonate across various demographics and cultures. Their flagship product is a sleep sack designed to help babies (and parents) sleep better.

### The Conception of 'BON'

Designing Bondle's brand identity began with an in-depth understanding of the parenting ecosystem. One of the initial challenges was navigating the founders' attachment to their original branding and guiding them towards a more progressive and inclusive narrative. The decision to rename the brand to "Bondle" was pivotal, signalling a fresh start and emphasizing the emotional connections the brand aims to foster. This renaming process involved extensive brainstorming, where keywords like "bond," "joy," and "bundle of happiness" inspired the final name.

For the visual identity, we analyzed multiple directions, balancing modernity and warmth. Our first approach was to create something playful and whimsical. That of course wasn't the solution we were looking for. After a few variations, we realized that even though Bondle was a baby care brand, it caters to parents. At its core, it is a brand about a community of parents.

### Introduction

Kaizen is about leaving spaces better than you found them — whether it's waste management or landscaping, it's the pursuit of perfection that sets us apart.

Kaizen is a brand built on the philosophy of continuous improvement, inspired by the Japanese ethos of refinement and betterment. "Kaizen" translates to "change for better" and encapsulates the brand's vision of elevating everyday services to a premium standard. At its core, Kaizen aims to disrupt traditionally viewed utilitarian industries by introducing luxury, precision, and sustainability elements. Rooted in values such as passion, persistence, and a commitment to perfection, Kaizen extends its expertise across a diverse portfolio that includes waste management and landscaping.

### A Delicate Balancing Act

One of the biggest challenges of this project was also the most significant ask in the brief we were given: creating a dynamic brand that feels premium. As an essential service, waste management is often undervalued, yet the workers who provide it are some of our most crucial. We understood the importance and meaningfulness of their work, and that understanding extended beyond just waste management. It speaks to the broader services Kaizen could offer and the overarching philosophy of improving and maintaining the community by "leaving it better than we found it."

The design process became an exploration of how to communicate "improvement" and "perfection" visually. The branding had to work across multiple touchpoints — from trucks and uniforms to digital interfaces and potential future sub-brands.

Kaizen is changing perceptions and transforming lives, one service at a time.

### Introduction

Mackenzie grew up with a simple but powerful lesson from her mom: Hope — live with purpose. So when it came to creating their favorite snack, it couldn't just check the 'healthy' boxes; it had to do more. It had to support gut health and make people feel their best. Eat Purposefully's granola stands out because of its unique, science-backed fermentation process, making it not just another health product but one with real benefits.

Naturally, when it was time for a brand overhaul, it had to stay true to this mission — rooted in science, purpose, and the homegrown identity that started it all.

The brand's name had to reflect this philosophy too. The renaming journey became a crucial part of the project — starting as Kenz Crunch, it evolved into Purposefully and later into Eat Purposefully due to trademark challenges and the need for broader brand adaptability.

> Color was one of the biggest debates. We initially leaned towards neutral and earthy tones, assuming they best conveyed health and organic qualities. But when we examined consumer psychology, we realized that while those tones suggest 'natural,' they often fail to create an emotional pull. On the other hand, bright colors immediately signal appetite and energy but risk feeling too 'fast food.' The challenge was to find a middle ground — colors that felt fresh and inviting while maintaining the credibility of a health-conscious brand.

### Introduction

Transactions reimagines the way we manage money, making it as natural as checking your messages. It simplifies financial tracking, just like opening a notebook and jotting things down, but with the intelligence and convenience of digital design.

### The Chandler Sister Cities Story

CSC approached us with a simple yet challenging brief: to create compelling collateral designs that enhance their outreach and communication efforts. Their focus was on tangible deliverables — brochures, digital assets, and event materials — that could better articulate their mission. However, as we dived deeper, it became clear that their existing branding lacked the cohesion needed to tie everything together seamlessly.

Despite its powerful mission, CSC's visual identity didn't quite match the depth of its impact. The original logo — a mechanized cogwheel — spoke only to one kind of progress: industrial and mechanical. While Chandler is a hub of innovation, its growth isn't just about machines and factories; it's about ideas, culture, and global connections.

### Logo: Honoring The Heritage of Chandler

As we began the rebranding process, we wanted to understand Chandler Sister Cities' mission, vision, and values, recognizing the true essence of what they stand for. Their commitment to nurturing global connections through cultural exchange and collaboration helped us grasp that their work was more than just about building relationships — it was about creating a legacy of mutual growth and shared values.

We wanted the logo to express progress and innovation while also paying homage to Chandler's geographical identity, incorporating motifs inspired by local landmarks. This led us to the 'Sun', a timeless symbol of hope and progress. The top half of the new logo incorporates the sun rise, representing fresh beginnings and the bright spirit of Chandler. The bottom half features arched bridge forms representing connection between communities.

> The Chandler Sister Cities icon is a distilled representation of connection, culture, and legacy. We wanted to move away from a purely mechanical interpretation and instead create something that felt human, dynamic, and rooted in Chandler's identity while adding contemporary values to it. The mark expands and moves fluidly as needed, reflecting the flexibility and inclusivity of the city's communities and culture.

### Introduction

OSHM's journey began with a bold vision: to make local Kirana stores digitally adept. The client brief focused on creating a platform to help these stores thrive in an increasingly competitive market dominated by tech-savvy giants. The client emphasized inclusivity, asking for a solution that required minimal technical know-how for the shopkeepers while being visually appealing and highly functional for customers.

### The Challenge of Designing Digital Platforms for Indian Audiences

Creating digital platforms for Indian audiences is as much about empathy as it is about design. It requires a deep understanding of cultural nuances, behavioral tendencies, and technical constraints.

OSHM was created as a digital platform to connect local Kirana stores with their neighborhood communities. It reimagined grocery shopping by equipping small store owners with tools to compete with larger e-commerce giants. Positioned as "Zomato for groceries," OSHM addressed the challenges of adapting to a digital world while maintaining the personal touch of local stores. The platform functioned as a two-way solution: enabling local stores to manage inventories and facilitate online purchases, while allowing customers to browse nearby stores, place orders, and choose between quick in-store pickup or home delivery.

**01 — Diversity in Digital Literacy:** India's digital landscape combines seasoned tech users and first-time internet adopters. This disparity in digital literacy requires an intuitive and easy platform. For OSHM, this meant simplifying the interface without compromising functionality.

**02 — Language and Accessibility:** India is a multilingual nation, and English, while widely understood in urban areas, is not the primary language for many users. Digital platforms need to support regional languages to ensure inclusivity.
